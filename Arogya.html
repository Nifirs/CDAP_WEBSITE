What is Arogya ?

The proposed solution Arogya, is to develop a Centralized Social Media Mobile Application platform that gives user the ability to identify and classify a group of selected important ayurvedic plant species in Sri Lanka, based on photographs of the plant’s leaf as well as other parts such as digested root and fruit, taken with mobile phone, to share information sources of them through information extraction, generate a quick summary on remedies and recipes for specific herbal plants. As well as user can add the tracked herbal plants locations with the aim of preservation of valuable Ayurvedic plants in Sri Lanka.

Literature Survey

Ayurvedic means a science of life and well-being with its unique approaches to social and spiritual life. Especially in Sri Lanka we have our own set of rare ayurvedic herbs which have been utilized by generations as medicinal treatments for a variety of diseases. Absence of specialists in this area makes proper identification as well as classification of valuable herbal plants a tedious task, which is essential for better treatment. There are existing applications which can identify plants with low prediction accuracies, as well as to give information regarding them. However, these applications are based on foreign plant data sets that do not include valuable herbs and shrubs with medicinal qualities.

  • Arogbase: Identify, Classify, Search Plants, Filter herbs
  • Plantex: Identify, Classify, Search Plants, Filter herbs
  • PlantSnap: Identify, Classify, Search Plants
  • Pl@ntNet: Identify, Classify, Search Plants
  • MedLeaf: Identify, Classify, Search Plants, Filter herbs

Research Gap

In Sri Lanka we have our own set of rare Ayurvedic herbs. But most of us are unable to identify these plants due to lack of knowledge. Dissemination of knowledge regarding herbal plants is restricted mainly to very limited group of people and is passed down from generation to generation who practice traditional medicine. So, recognizing these endemic herbal plants is a challenging problem in the fields of Ayurvedic medicine, computer vision, and machine learning, because although leaf classification apps have been implemented for leaves in other countries, such an automated app with a reliable identification and classification mechanism has not still been implemented for the purpose of locals. There are existing applications which can identify plants with low prediction accuracies. However, these applications are based on foreign plant data sets that do not include valuable herbs and shrubs with medicinal qualities. Therefore, it is the main research gap which can be identified through the existing research and implemented automated applications regarding plant identification and classification.

Research problem

  • Lack of technological research carried out on the classification of Ayurvedic herbs using machine learning and computer vision.
  • Absence of a single platform to detect and classify ayurvedic leaves, give important information about them and to show the locations from where those plants can be found.
  • Lack of a system which identifies the category type of disease in an unknown herbal remedy by giving its special characteristics and which displays the most used herbal sample of each category and visualizing some of herbal remedies that are included.
  • Lacks use of a common GIS platform, while only manual location records are available currently.
  • Dissemination of knowledge regarding herbal plants is restricted mainly to very limited group of people and is passed down from generation to generation who only practice traditional medicine.

Research objective

  • Main Objective

    • The proposed solution is to develop a Centralized Social Media platform that gives user the ability to identify and classify a group of selected important ayurvedic plant species in Sri Lanka, based on photographs of the plant’s leaf as well as other parts such as digested root and fruit, taken with mobile phone.


  • Specific Objectives
    • Recognition and classification of herbs used for Ayurvedic treatment once the user inputs an image of a plant part captured by mobile camera
    • Display onsite information on the identified medicinal plant and generate a summary on the medicinal values
    • Identification of the category type of disease in an unknown herbal remedy by giving its special characteristics
    • Adding the tracked locations of herbal plants into the map

Methodology

This system contains four main components. Each component uses different Deep Learning, Machine Learning Algorithms, NLP and Image Processing Techniques.

  • Plant Detection: Selecting the most accurate and the highest performance segmentation technique using experimental outcomes
  • Plant Classification: Using transfer learning based on deep CNN using a mobile application in an offline approach
    Achieved Testing Accuracy:99.5%
  • Plant Information Summarization: Web page Info Extraction, Seq2Seq LSTM model with Attention Mechanism
    Achieved Testing Accuracy:98.6%
  • Plant Geographical Location Mapping: GIS technology
  • Analyze the predicted type of diseases & Identify ayurvedic plant related posts: Machine Learning, Image OCR techniques and cosine similarity
    Achieved Testing Accuracy:95.0%
